About

Bio

Dr. Mukesh Pandey is a physics researcher currently at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR). He completed his M.Sc. in Physics at the University of Delhi (2017) and his Ph.D. in Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology Ropar (2024), with a thesis on mechanical instabilities in the blistering and buckling of polymer-supported two-dimensional materials.

He subsequently worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Materials Science and Engineering, Uppsala University, where his work centered on nanoengineering — strain and defect control — of graphene and other 2D materials, with applications spanning straintronics, nanoelectronics, and gas sensing.

Research interests

What I work on

Rhombohedral graphene & artificial moire' superlattices

Flat-Band Engineering of Strongly Correlated Quantum and Topological States in Rhombohedral Graphene.

2D material adhesion mechanics

Adhesion mechanics, blistering, and buckling instabilities in polymer-supported graphene and MoS₂ multilayers.

Strain engineering & straintronics

Local and global straining techniques to tune the electronic, vibrational, and optical properties of 2D materials for flexible device applications.

Defect engineering

Electron-beam and focused-ion-beam induced structural modification — atomically thin domes, controlled defects — in graphene and transition-metal dichalcogenides.

Viscous fingering & blister dynamics

Confined-fluid instabilities and interfacial phenomena in spontaneously formed 2D-material blisters.

2D heterostructures & sensing

2D material heterostructures and nanoelectronic/gas-sensor applications built on engineered strain and defects.
